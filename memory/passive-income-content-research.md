### How YouTube Ad Revenue Works (CPM vs RPM)

**Key Metrics:**
- **CPM (Cost Per Mille)**: What advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions. Average CPMs range from $1-15 depending on niche.
- **RPM (Revenue Per Mille)**: What YOU earn per 1,000 views. Typically 45-55% of CPM (YouTube takes ~45-55% cut).
- **Average creator RPM**: $1-5 per 1,000 views (very niche-dependent)

**Real Numbers:**
- 100,000 views/month â‰ˆ $50-500/month (general niche)
- 100,000 views/month â‰ˆ $300-1,500/month (high-paying niche: finance, business, tech)
- 1M views/month â‰ˆ $500-5,000+/month

**Factors Affecting Revenue:**
1. Niche (finance pays 10-20x more than entertainment)
2. Audience location (US/UK/Canada = highest CPMs)
3. Content length (longer videos = more ads = higher RPM)
4. Seasonality (Q4 = highest ad rates)

### YouTube Shorts Monetization (2025-2026)

**Current Requirements:**
- 1,000 subscribers + 10M views (90 days) OR
- 4,000 watch hours (standard program)

**Revenue:**
- Creator Pool: Shorts ads generate revenue into a pool, distributed to creators based on views (not full ad revenue)
- Typical rate: $0.01-0.06 per view (much lower than long-form)
- Most creators earn 10-20% of what long-form generates

**Strategic Use:**
- Shorts for discovery â†’ long-form for revenue
- Use Shorts to funnel to evergreen long-form videos

### Affiliate Links in YouTube Descriptions

**How It Works:**
- Add links in first 48 hours (before algorithm hides them)
- Use redirect/tracking links (Bitly, Genius Links)
- Disclose affiliate relationships

**Best Performing Niches:**
- Tech/gadgets (Amazon Associates)
- Tools/software (SaaS affiliate programs)
- Books (Amazon)
- Courses/education

**Typical Earnings:**
- Amazon: 1-10% commission
- Software: 20-50% recurring commission
- Average YouTuber: 5-15% of total revenue from affiliates

### Mediavine vs AdSense Thresholds

| Ad Network | Minimum Traffic | Avg RPM | Payment |
|------------|------------------|---------|---------|
| **AdSense** | None | $1-10 | Direct |
| **Mediavine** | 50K sessions/month | $15-40 | Direct |
| **AdThrive** | 100K views/month | $20-50 | Direct |
| **Raptive** | 100K+ views/month | $20-60 | Higher |

**Recommendation:**
- Start: AdSense (or Ezoic, Media.net)
- 50K sessions: Apply to Mediavine
- 100K+ views: Switch to AdThrive or Raptive

**RPM by Niche (AdSense baseline):**
- Finance: $15-30
- Tech: $10-20
- Lifestyle: $5-15
- General: $3-10

### Affiliate Marketing for Blogs

**Amazon Associates:**
- 1-10% commission (category-dependent)
- 24-hour cookie (Extended to 72h for Prime items in 2025)
- Good for product reviews

**High-Ticket Affiliates (20-50%):**
- ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign (email marketing)
- Webflow, Squarespace (website builders)
- Coursera, Skillshare (courses)
- Shopify (e-commerce)

**Strategy:**
- Product review posts = highest conversion
- Comparison posts ("Best X for Y")
- Tutorial posts with embedded product links

---

### SEO Strategies for Blogs

**On-Page Essentials:**
1. Target long-tail keywords (1,000-5,000 monthly searches)
2. Optimize title tags (primary keyword first)
3. Write comprehensive, in-depth content (2,000+ words for competitive topics)
4. Internal linking between related posts
5. Fast site speed (Core Web Vitals matter)

**Content Strategy:**
- "Skyscraper method": Find top content, create better
- Update existing content regularly
- Focus on informational keywords (how, what, best)
- Avoid thin content

**Backlinks:**
- Guest posting (still works)
- Digital PR
- Resource page link building
- HARO/Connectively responses

### WordPress vs Substack vs Medium

| Platform | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| **WordPress** | Full control, SEO, monetization | Hosting/maintenance needed | Serious bloggers, long-term |
| **Substack** | Built-in audience, email list, easy | Limited design, 10% fee | Newsletter-focused, writers |
| **Medium** | Built-in audience, easy | Low monetization, no email list | Testing ideas, reach |

**Recommendation for Passive Income:**
- **WordPress self-hosted** = Full control, best SEO, no platform risk
- **Substack** = Good for building email list + newsletter revenue
- **Medium** = Best for reach, not for monetization

**Hybrid Approach:** Publish on Medium (reach) â†’ Drive to email list â†’ Monetize independently
